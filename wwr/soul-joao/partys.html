Partys!

Campina Grande, the city known for hosting the largest São João in the world, offers a unique festive experience during this time of year. During the month of June, the parties in Campina Grande are at their peak, bringing joy, music and dance to the streets.

The parties of São João in Campina Grande are known for their contagious and lively atmosphere. People gather to celebrate June traditions, wearing traditional costumes, such as the famous quadrilhas, with their colorful costumes and choreographed dances. Music is a central element of these festivals, with forró bands and local artists entertaining the public with their live performances.

The shows at the Parque do Povo in Campina Grande are known as one of the city's greatest highlights. During the month of June, when the traditional June festival takes place, the park is transformed into a real stage for great musical performances, attracting thousands of people from all over the country. The Parque do Povo is a large, wooded area, designed to house a complete structure for the Campina Grande June Festival. With a capacity to receive hundreds of thousands of people, the park is the stage for shows by renowned artists of Brazilian music, especially forró and sertanejo. During the festival days, the Parque do Povo is divided into sectors, such as the Main Stage, where the main shows take place, and the alternative stages, where local and regional artists perform. There is also a large food area, with stalls selling typical June food, such as corn, canjica, and pamonha, among others. The shows at Parque do Povo are marked by a lively and contagious atmosphere, with the public dancing to the sound of the music and participating in June quadrilhas (square dancing). It is common to see couples dancing forró, dressed in traditional costumes, and people of all ages having fun to the contagious rhythm of the party. Among the artists who have performed at Parque do Povo are names like Elba Ramalho, Wesley Safadão, Simone e Simaria, Gusttavo Lima, Xand Avião, and other great exponents of Brazilian music.
