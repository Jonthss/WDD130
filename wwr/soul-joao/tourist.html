Campina Grande's Tourist Spots

The Tropeiros da Borborema is a tourist attraction located in Campina Grande, in Paraíba, Brazil. The site is a tribute to the old troopers, merchants who, in past centuries, traveled through the region transporting goods on donkeys and horses.

The Tropeiros da Borborema monument is a sculpture depicting a muleteer mounted on his pack animal, carrying bales of cotton. The statue, made of bronze, represents the historical and cultural importance of these characters in the region.

The Açude Velho is one of the most famous tourist attractions in Campina Grande, located in the state of Paraíba, Brazil. It is an artificial lake that has become an important historical and cultural landmark of the city.

Built in 1865, the Açude Velho was initially designed to supply the city of Campina Grande with drinking water. Over time, it became a place of coexistence and leisure for the local residents, and today it is one of the main tourist attractions in the region.

The lake has an extensive green area around it, with beautiful gardens, benches, and spaces for walks and physical exercise. It is a place frequented by families, couples, and friends who wish to enjoy a peaceful and pleasant environment.

Besides its natural beauty, the Açude Velho also hosts several cultural events throughout the year, such as musical concerts, handicraft fairs, and gastronomic festivals. During the traditional Campina Grande June Festival, the lake becomes the stage for quadrilha presentations, attracting tourists from all over the country.
